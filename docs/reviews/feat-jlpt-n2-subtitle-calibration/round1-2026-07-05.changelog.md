# Round 1 Changelog: JLPT N2 Subtitle Calibration

## Scope

Rebuild the July 2024 N2 listening subtitles around complete sentences,
speaker-turn boundaries, and audible pauses, then regenerate all 29 exercise
clips.

- `2024еєі07жЬИN2еРђеКЫйЯ≥йҐС.raw.srt` preserves the original 342 coarse ASR
  segments and their real-audio timing anchors.
- `202407.txt` contains 597 reviewed text fragments with substantially better
  punctuation and wording, but its timeline belongs to a different edit and
  includes a one-minute timestamp error from source cue 470 onward.
- `202407.mp3` remains the playback source.

Neither subtitle source was safe to use directly: the raw SRT crossed speaker
turns and combined paragraphs, while the reviewed transcript split sentences
into short phrases and could not be copied onto the recording timeline.

## Calibration

`scripts/calibrate-listening-subtitles.py` now:

1. Applies reviewed wording corrections.
2. Joins short phrase fragments until a complete sentence or response is
   formed.
3. Preserves numbered prompts and answer choices as semantic units.
4. Splits long sentences only at Japanese comma pauses, with a 44-character
   target ceiling.
5. Aligns the reviewed and raw text streams with a 0.967 similarity ratio.
6. Uses 439 reliable text/timing points and 1,121 detected audio pauses to map
   boundaries onto the real recording.
7. Rejects overlaps, non-positive durations, cues over 18 seconds, cues over 76
   normalized characters, and stale generated output.

The calibrated SRT contains 511 cues. The 29 exercise clips contain 402 of
those cues; instructions and breaks outside question boundaries remain
excluded.

## Generator Hardening

`scripts/build-listening-exam.mjs` no longer depends on fixed SRT cue IDs.
Question boundaries are located by expected marker text near known audio
positions. This allows sentence splitting and merging without silently
shifting clip boundaries.

The generator runs subtitle staleness validation first, includes cues that
cross a clip boundary with clamped relative times, and verifies every generated
MP3 against its subtitle timeline.
